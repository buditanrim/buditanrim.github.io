2019 - 2020

Improving design system contribution experience

The hesitation to contribute

Bazaar design system helps Bukalapak to speed up the groundwork. However, our system was not evolving as it serves 40+ products across the company. We observed a lot of designers were hesitant to contribute. We’ll see designers are either using any available components or create their custom components but never talked to design systems. We wonder why.

My role

I led the design systems team. In this project, I empowered the designer to guide the discovery process with frameworks, help communicate this company-wide, and be involved in the strategy discussion of the overall concept to improve the contribution experience.

The challenge

As we established design systems, it lacked contributions and was not evolving.

The outcome

An enjoyable contribution experience and increases 6x of contribution quarter by quarter

Big question #1

How might we encourage people to contribute to the system?

The discovery

Early insights of why people hesitant to contribute

We conducted an interview with 3 engineers and 3 designers, trying to understand their interaction with design systems in their daily basis. Here are the findings I can share:

1

Unaware if they can contribute

Designers and engineers didn’t know if they can contribute. Many express they are more than willing to contribute, but didn’t know it is possible.

2

Tight deadline

Designers pressured with the tight deadlines. Most of them hesitant to engage with us because they afraid it will slow them down.

3

Public forum discussion is too confronting

Designers hesitate to ask a question in our Slack channel because the design systems team usually asked them back with the intention to gain context. However, this interaction make them uncomfortable.

4

Little design exploration

Often, designers committed to their solution before exploring other options. Resulting an unoptimal solution for the certain problem.

Framing the problem

Dismantling the insights

These insights make us more aware that system users are already busy with their work and need a minimum effort to engage with design systems.

We mapped the problems and dismantle it using problem-tree framework to map the why and the consequence of each problem. In summary, we need to provide a safe environment between designers and system designers to exchange perspective, discussing rationale, and explore together.

Exploring the concept

We generate a lot of ideas with post-it. There is one particular idea that our team excited about. Influenced by our team’s culture who love to discuss complex things on the whiteboard. The discussion by the end of the activity looks like this:

What if...
system users can be in front of the whiteboard with us?
So...
they can explain their goal and the context we need
Yes, and...
they can book it whenever they need it
Also
let’s keep the group small so they’re comfortable.
Our team make a preliminary to-be-scenario with the system users.

Testing the concept

It’s time to test our concept. We looked for product designers who just started their project and are willing to try this. Three designers signed up. During the session, one of the designers from the design system team will partner with the product designer trying to solve a specific problem.

The reaction was positive.

The discussion is much more effective, one of the outputs that I love is the product designer realized that it’s not the component is the problem, but tweaking the flow and the layout could’ve solved the problem better. It seems the concept of having a sparring partner is worth it.

I started to understand what to consider when creating a component and the importance to keep it scalable. I really love this collaboration ground. — Designer
This session is super fun! It really bring us closer together, now I feel like we’re all Bazaar designers. — Designer

Rollout and Impact

Introducing Collaboration Ground

The validation gave us confident to roll this out. We started to tweak a few part of the overall experience based on the learnings too. We called this experience Collaboration Ground, we wanted to emphasize the collaboration part because we learned that is what the designers value the most throughout the exercise.

Positive impact

Collaboration ground had a positive impact, we increased the number of engagement, we had 28 total sign ups components being contributed just in a quarter, the design systems start to evolve, and we started to have better relationship with system users.

Number of engagement increases, 28 signups in total for the last three quarters

500-600% increases in contribution quarter by quarter

16 contributions to improve existing patterns or components

8 contributions to add new patterns or components

To be continued: After we launched the Collaboration Ground, we get continuous flow of contribution. We still need to organize and scale the system better for 8 different business units who maintain 40 product portfolios.
